## What JASE actually covers (from analyzing the live site)
JASE (Journal of Agricultural Sciences and Engineering, Sami Publishing Co., launched 2019
with the Biosafety Society of Iran) is a quarterly, open-access, double-blind peer-reviewed
journal. It does NOT specialize narrowly — real published articles span: crop
salinity/abiotic stress trials, soil quality indexing (Vis-NIR spectroscopy), insect pest
biology under contaminant stress, fisheries stock management, herbicidal/weed studies,
ethnobotany, and biodiversity/genetic resource policy. No APC, free to publish, Web of
Science-profile-oriented, iThenticate plagiarism screening.

Conclusion: to compete with/match this, your journal needs breadth across agricultural
science generally, not a narrow niche — the 100 topics below are organized into the actual
categories JASE publishes in, so any journal launched on your platform in this field has a
credible, full first content calendar from day one.

## How to use this list
- Bracketed placeholders like `[regional staple crop]` or `[region]` — fill with whatever
  actual geographic/crop focus your first journal targets. Leaving them generic makes the
  topic usable for any journal instance on the platform.
- Feed each numbered topic straight into `ARTICLE_WRITING_PROMPT.md` one at a time — for a
  research journal you'd want these written as literature-review-style knowledge articles
  (not primary research, since you have no lab data), clearly framed as reviews/overviews
  citing real published studies, not presented as original research.
- Categories 4, 2, and 7 (entomology, soil science, animal science) had the most real
  published-article evidence on the live site — weight your first batch of articles there
  if you want maximum topical overlap with what JASE's own audience already searches for.
