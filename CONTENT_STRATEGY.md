# CONTENT_STRATEGY.md — Tech New Publication (technewpublication.com)

> You don't need deep domain knowledge to use this — it's written so the AI applies these
> rules automatically. You only ever supply a topic.

## 1. Purpose
Tech New Publication covers technology news and knowledge for a general tech-interested
reader — not a niche developer audience, not a consumer gadget-unboxing site. Every article
should leave the reader either informed about something current, or able to make a decision
(which product, which tool, which approach) they couldn't confidently make before.

## 2. Content pillars (default set — the AI should slot every topic into one of these)
- **Pillar 1 — Breaking/Current Tech News:** product launches, major company announcements,
  AI model releases, policy/regulation affecting tech. Time-sensitive, published fast.
- **Pillar 2 — Explainers & How Things Work:** "what is X", "how does Y actually work" —
  evergreen, ranks long-term, written for someone smart but unfamiliar with the term.
- **Pillar 3 — Comparisons & Buying Decisions:** "X vs Y", "best [tool/device] for [use case]",
  "how to choose a...". Highest commercial/decision intent.
- **Pillar 4 — Guides & Tutorials:** step-by-step, practical, "how to do X" — builds authority
  and gets long session times.
- **Pillar 5 — Industry Trends & Analysis:** where a technology/market is heading, informed
  opinion backed by data — builds the publication's authority and gets backlinks.

If a topic doesn't clearly fit one pillar, the AI should pick the closest fit and say which
one it chose.

## 3. Non-negotiable article requirements
- Every article states, in the first 2-3 sentences, exactly what the reader will know or be
  able to decide by the end — no throat-clearing intro.
- At least one internal link to a related pillar-1-5 article or a relevant service/product
  page on the site (the AI should propose the link target even if it doesn't exist yet —
  flag it as "link candidate" so it can be created later).
- Every factual/statistical claim traces to a real, named, linked source.
- No fabricated benchmarks, pricing, specs, or release dates — verify or state "unconfirmed."
- Tech content ages fast — every article should include the date context ("as of [month/year]")
  for anything version/price/spec-dependent, since it will be read long after publishing.

## 4. Tone / voice
- Direct, confident, no hedging filler ("it could be argued that...").
- Technical terms explained on first use, not assumed.
- No generic AI-blog tells: no "In today's fast-paced digital world," no "In conclusion,"
  no restating the title as the closing sentence.
- Opinion/analysis pieces (Pillar 5) can take a clear position — hedge on facts, not on judgment.

## 5. Internal linking & structure rules
- 3-5 internal links per article, naturally placed, never a forced "related articles" dump
  in the middle of a paragraph.
- Comparison/buying-decision articles (Pillar 3) always end with a clear recommendation, not
  just "it depends" — take a stance based on the stated use case.
- Explainers (Pillar 2) link forward to at least one Pillar 3 or Pillar 4 article — this is
  the site's main conversion path (curious reader → decision-ready reader).

## 6. What "best/updated" means for this site
Prioritize sources and facts from the last 6-12 months for anything version/price/feature
specific. Older sources are fine for foundational explainers but should be flagged as such
rather than presented as current.
